100 days of Trump, plenty of Bay Area protests
By Michael Bodley and Emma O'Neill
<%= t.include("partials/_title_social.html") %>
Even for the Bay Area, long the United States' hotbed of progressive activism, the first 100 days of the Trump administration brought a level of protest not seen in generations. Thousands of people who had never in their lives held a picket sign or bellowed “this is what democracy looks like” marched through the streets of San Francisco, Oakland, Berkeley, San Jose and other Bay Area cities to protest the policy prescriptions championed by Trump. It’s called the resistance, and it’s not going away.
